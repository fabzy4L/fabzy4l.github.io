01

The Problem

Biological therapeutics — CAR-T cells, viral vectors, iPSC-derived therapies — are among the most complex products humans have ever manufactured. A single patient dose can take weeks, require hundreds of process steps, and fail catastrophically from a deviation that no one caught in time.

The manufacturing intelligence needed to run these processes safely and at scale does not exist as software. It lives in the heads of scientists and operators who are retiring, burning out, or moving on.

No institutional memory
Critical process knowledge is undocumented or siloed in individuals. When operators leave, the knowledge leaves with them.
Deviation blindness
Process deviations are identified days or weeks after they occur — too late to intervene, triggering costly investigations and batch failures.
Regulatory drag
GMP compliance documentation is manual, error-prone, and consumes disproportionate operator time that should go to process improvement.
Scale barrier
Processes validated at small scale break at commercial scale because no system captures what actually drives product quality.
02

What We're Building

LabOps.AI is an AI-native intelligence platform that captures, structures, and activates the process knowledge inside a biomanufacturing facility — connecting batch records, equipment data, deviations, and regulatory history into a unified operational brain.

CQA / CPP Extraction
Process Intelligence Engine
Automatically identifies Critical Quality Attributes and Critical Process Parameters from unstructured batch records, SOPs, and deviation reports.
Real-Time Monitoring
Deviation Detection
Surfaces process anomalies before they become failures — giving operators and QA the window to intervene rather than investigate.
Knowledge Architecture
Institutional Memory
Structures tacit operator knowledge into retrievable, auditable records that survive personnel turnover and scale across sites.
GMP Compliance
Regulatory Readiness
Documentation workflows designed for FDA/EMA audit environments — reducing compliance overhead and accelerating batch release.
